Assessment

Assessments theoretically serve a purpose of measuring how much you have learned (i.e. how much you have achieved of the course goals). I would recommend you to first focus on your learning, then grades will follow. I hope you can focus on your own learning. It is my intent to design assessments that are a source of intellectual curiosity, joy, and learning rather than a source of stress. The timezone of this course is PDT.

Attendance and Participation

This course is taught asynchronously and synchronously which means that you will learn the content of the course by watching videos at a time that is convenient for you and attend zoom session at the scheduled times. For the zoom session my expectation is that not only you attend but also participate actively in discussions by asking or answering questions.

Problem Sets

Problem sets are an opportunity to practice the topics learned in the lecture videos. They are graded for completion and effort. Incorrect solutions are acceptable but incomplete solutions are not. I hold the right to decide if enough effort is put into solving the problem. You can work on problem sets individually or in groups but each one of you needs to submit separately. Working together means discussing reasoning for questions. You need to write your solutions independently from one another. We will use synchronous sessions to get in groups and work together. Synchronous sessions are not a good time to start problem sets. You should attempt problem sets before coming on zoom. The problem sets are assigned at the beginning of the week and are due Wednesday at 09:00 pm.

Midterm Exam

Midterm exam will be timed. The midterm exam is scheduled during the synchronous session on August 16.

Final Exam

The final exam will have two parts, written and oral. The written exam is scheduled during the synchronous session on September 1. The oral exam will need to be scheduled individually for the week of Aug 30 - September 3. Scheduling instructions will be shared later.

Late work
The pace of the summer session is so fast that late work would make you fall behind drastically. The work is expected to be submitted on time. However if there is any acceptable reason (e.g. religious holiday, atheletic competition etc.) please communicate with me in advance regarding accommodations.

Writing an email

While communicating over email, please make sure the email
  • has an informative subject line.
  • articulates your question or problem well.
  • has a closing statement such as Sincerely, Kind Regards etc.
  • has your name at the end.
Here is a sample email:
Subject: Stats 115 - Your Subject

Dear Dr. Dogucu,
I hope you are doing well (or some other humane sentence). I am writing to you regarding ______________. (Your concern or problem) (Any explanations, requests or suggestions for solutions to your problem)

Sincerely/Kind regards,
Your name

Online communication

Most of our online comminication will take place on ed. While communicating online please note the following:
  • Before posting a question, please make sure that the same question has not been posted before.
  • ed posts are labeled as Week 1, 2, 3.. and so on. Make sure to write your post at the appropriate week's folder.
  • If you choose to post memes or any jokes, be mindful. Any joke about a person, or a group of people, or cultures is not funny. However statistics jokes are always welcome.
  • Respect opinions of others
  • There are absolutely no stupid questions. Do not feel shy about asking questions. Do not judge anyone about any question that they ask.
  • If somebody asks a question about homework etc. do not reveal answers for them. Instead, show them in which lecture we have covered the topic.
  • Be generous with likes. That means when you read something useful like the post so that we all know that we are not talking to ourselves but to each other.
